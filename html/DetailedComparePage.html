The grades to the above criteria have been given on the following basis:

CRIME RATING:
Neighborhoods with crime rate below 10% are graded A+. Neighborhoods with crime rate between 10-20%,are given an A grade. Neighborhoods with crime rate between 20-30%, given a B+ grade. Neighborhoods with crime rate between 30-40%,are given a B grade. Neighborhoods with crime rate between 40-50%, are given a C grade.Neighborhoods with crime rate 50% and above are given a D grade.


SCHOOL RATING:
Neighborhoods with a school rating equal to 10 are graded A+. Neighborhoods with a school rating of 9 are graded A. Neighborhoods with a school rating of 8 are graded B+. Neighborhoods with a school rating of 7 are graded B.Neighborhoods with a school rating of 6 are graded C.Neighborhoods with a school rating of 5 and below are graded D.


TRANSPORT RATING:
Neighborhoods with very good public transportation are given A+ grade. Neighborhoods with good public transportation are given an A grade, with average public transportation are given a B grade, with below average public transportation are given a B grade.


HOUSE PRICE:
Lower the average house price, the higher will be the grade.


AVERAGE INCOME:
If the average income of people living in that neighborhood is above 700K, the grade given is A+. If it is between 50-70K, the grade given is A else the grade given is B.
